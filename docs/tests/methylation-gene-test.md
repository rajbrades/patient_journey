# Methylation Genetic Test (MGT)

## Overview

A genetic test focused on five key actionable genes within the methylation cycle — genes that can be directly supported through supplementation. Unlike ancestry tests (e.g., 23andMe), the MGT does not provide heritage or physical trait information. Instead, it delivers insights into how the body processes essential raw materials through these five genes and how targeted supplements can optimize that process.

## What It Tests

- Evaluates **5 genetic markers** in the methylation pathway
- Genes are specifically chosen for their actionability (can be supported via supplementation)

## Deliverables

- Comprehensive **self-guided 10-page report** with results
- Option to purchase a **2-month supply of supplements** based on results

## Health Goals / Use Cases

- Optimizing cellular energy and nutrient processing
- Understanding genetic factors affecting mood, focus, and cognitive function
- Personalized supplement planning
- Longevity and preventive health
- Hormonal health support (methylation affects estrogen metabolism)
- Cardiovascular risk awareness (MTHFR + homocysteine connection)

## Key Genes Evaluated

The MGT covers 5 genes within the methylation pathway (exact gene panel to be confirmed, typically includes variants such as):

- **MTHFR** — folate metabolism, homocysteine regulation
- **COMT** — dopamine/estrogen breakdown, mood and stress response
- **MTR / MTRR** — B12 utilization
- **BHMT** — alternative methylation pathway
